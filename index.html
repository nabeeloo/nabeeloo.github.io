Business simulations

Creating strategic client conversations based on industry disruptors

Nabeel pioneered an experiential, social-collaborative and competitive business simulation focused on industry disruptors. This differs from normal business simulations in two ways:

1) Focus is on strategic client conversations, not only running a business, and

2) Teams comprise different company profiles to realistically represent the current marketplace, as opposed to all companies with the same profile

Result Participants rated program among the highest of 50+ similar learning offerings
Result Creation of 5 — and counting — unique business simulations by industry
Result Nearly 1,000 participants in 2018 alone

Neuroscience of trusted relationships

Deriving practical business insights from neuroscience research

Nabeel conceived of a new way to use neuroscience to inform building trusted business relationships. Nabeel’s team developed a video series with Wharton Neuroscience Initiative director/professor Dr Michael Platt.

Videos are based on recent brain imaging studies that suggest our brain is wired to be social. To build good relationships, we need to understand how to tap into that "social brain" network to maximize connectivity between individuals.

Result Most sought after learning content related to business development
Result Invited talks and published article on the collaboration and video series
Result Ongoing relationship with Dr Platt/Wharton to create a "Neuroscience of ..." series

Behavior change

Transferring behavioral skills to the workplace

Nabeel leveraged 60 years of behavioral-modeling research to create learning videos designed for behavior change. Rooted in the principles of Bandura’s social cognitive theory and delivered through video scenarios, mastery modeling is among the most powerful ways to build learner self-efficacy and workplace skills.

Result Learners report a 75% higher likelihood of application in job role
Result 55% increased confidence level to apply learning

Learning analytics

Collect data. Classify data. Benefit from data.

Nabeel uses a 3-tier model to improve learning through analytics:

1) Use of predictive factors and artificial intelligence (AI) for aggregate analysis of learning data to inform what topics are ripe for a learning intervention

2) Treating the learning experience like a product, focused on correlating user flow for optimal learning design.

3) Getting in the "black box" of the learning asset itself, using metrics akin to website analytics to improve the quality of the learning experience.

Result 50% more accurate justification of topics to focus on for learning
Result 80% faster ability to customize learning campaign goals via web analytics tools
Result 30% improved learning design and earlier identification of potential design remediations
